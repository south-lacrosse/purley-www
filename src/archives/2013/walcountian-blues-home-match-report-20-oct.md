# 2-0 (Matt P,Matt C), 4-3 (Billy,Matt C), 5-4 (Matt P), 6-5 (Billy)
---

The Purley pitch was quite heavy after two days of rain but the team
started positively. The boys were moving the ball well and controlling the
pace of the game - with a goal each from the two Matts, Payne and Carey,
leading to a first quarter score of 2-0. After tough opening games against
Hampstead and Spencer this was a much closer contest. As the second quarter
progressed both teams shared an equal amount of possession but good
defensive play kept the score low. Half way through the quarter Purley gave
up two cheap goals, but managed to maintain their slender lead to end the
quarter 4-3.

In the third quarter the 10 men of Purley started to tire, but the midfield
trio of Mike Barrett, Guy Francis and Sam Baranowski kept giving
everything. A pretty special crease dive goal by Matt Payne was the
classiest moment of the quarter. The Purley defence continued to
communicate effectively which restricted the Blues to low percentage shots.

The final quarter started slowly with both teams giving the ball away
cheaply, but mistakes weren't being converted into goals. The purple men
managed to maintain a two goal lead until the closing moments when the
Blues pulled one back to make it a one goal game. The Blues had possession
with 30 seconds to go but failed to get a good look at the cage and Purley
cleared the ball with seconds remaining to progress through to the next
round of the Flags. Final score 6-5.

**Team:** Goal: Paul Terry; Defence: Andy Booth, Dave Copple, Dave
Slaughter; Midfield: Mike Barrett (2), Guy Francis, Sam Baranowski; Attack:
Matt Payne (2), Matt Carey (2), Andy Davis \
Ref: Justin Baranowski
