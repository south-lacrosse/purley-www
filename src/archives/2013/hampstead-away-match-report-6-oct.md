Purley started the 2012/13 season with a difficult away fixture against
Hampstead, last year's champions. Due to early season availability
problems, Purley were only able to field ten players - but, the team was
excited and ready to get the new year underway.

The team started strongly with Mike Barrett winning the first face-off.
This was as far ahead as the team were going to get. Despite some strong
goalkeeping from Paul Terry, Hampstead opened the scoring and went several
goals ahead. Purley fought back and scored themselves before the end of the
first quarter, going into the break at 3-1 down.

The second quarter was a tour de force by Purley. The team played some
exceptional team defence and held the ball well in attack. The exact score
has been lost to the mists of time, but the team went into the half time
break with smiles on faces and heads held high. Early on in the second half
Purley managed to claw the difference back to one goal - for the best part
of a minute. At this point Hampstead's substitutes began to tell. The
dastardly opposition pulled a sneaky trick in trying to exploit the tiring
legs of the Purley Lions by pressuring the ball and moving quickly on the
break. How dare they.

Hampstead pulled slightly further away in the last quarter and the game
finished 11-6. Purley fought hard and can be proud of the score, under
difficult circumstances the team did incredibly well and played some great
lacrosse. It the same level of performance and desire is kept up the South
Premiership should pose no problems. Special mention goes to Jay and Sam
Baranowski - these two young players made their debuts for the club and did
exceptionally well. For two players with such little experience to hold
their own against a team of ex-college players speaks volumes about their
potential (#onestowatch). Also, congratulations to Guy Francis for scoring
on his return to lacrosse.
